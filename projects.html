Research Projects

Construction of Novel Metal-Organic Frameworks and Applications in Catalysis

Metal-organic frameworks (MOFs), as unique porous materials built from metal ions or clusters (“nodes”) and organic ligands (“linkers”), are widely explored in the area of heterogeneous catalysis. The porous structures of MOFs can enrich substrates around the catalytic sites and these defined pores also dispaly size-selection at the same time. Additionally, as heterogeneous catalysts, MOFs can be easily separated from the reaction mixtures, simplifying the recycling procedure and decreasing the cost. We aim to design and construct novel MOF structures using polycarboxylic acid ligands based on the bulky aromatic ring skeleton with a large conjugated π-system and explore their applications as catalysts for CO2 conversion.

Development of Competent Molecular Electrocatalysts for Carbon Dioxide Reduction

The aim is to develop novel and improved molecular electrocatalysts, utilizing earth-abundant metals, for small molecule activation and catalysis in the area of energy research and green chemistry. Our interest lies in the interactions between transition metal centers and ligand spheres in the catalytic carbon dioxide reduction. Inspired by the elegant design of active sites in metalloenzymes, we will employ these strategies, with specific understandings of steps and reactive intermediates involved in the catalytic turnover from both kinetic and thermodynamic perspectives by various analytic techniques, to provide a framework for the rational design of highly effective environmental-friendly catalysts.

Melting and Vitrification Behavior of Porous Materials

To date, the field of MOFs and coordination polymers (CPs), has focused on the crystalline, ordered state. However, MOFs and CPs demonstrate a range of interesting physical behaviors, which are closely related to non-crystalline systems, such as amorphous solids, glasses, and liquids. The observation and control of disorder and defects in are of vital importance when investigating metastable characteristics in the structures and examining functions as catalysts, conductors, etc. This area of research has been growing and is expected to be explored due to the largely unknown properties of the liquid phases and glasses obtained by various techniques. Our aim is to design a new class of porous materials, exploring their fundamental phase-changing behaviors and responsiveness to external stimuli, as well as finding potential applications.
