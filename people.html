About Me

I am currently pursuing my Bachelor of Science in Chemistry (Poling Class, Project of Elite Student for Fundamental Disciplines in China) working with professor Bin Zhao at Nankai University (expected June 2021). My undergrad research, supported by College of Chemistry and Nankai University, concentrates on the design, synthesis, and characterization of porous materials, in particular, the luminescent metal-organic frameworks (MOFs) for detecting antibiotics in different solvent systems.

In summer 2019, I went to work with professor V. Sara Thoi in the Department of Chemistry at Johns Hopkins University as a visiting undergrad researcher. There I got familiar with the development of competent molecular catalysts for electrochemical CO2 reduction. Particularly, we investigated the redox properties of one novel rhenium tricarbonyl complex and its catalytic responses to targeted reactions.
